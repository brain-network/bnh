More about

The theme of the hackathon will broadly cover a variety of topics related to network science tools for neuroscience/imaging research, including code development, data modeling and analysis, as well as results interpretation.

The goal of Brainhack Networks is to bring together researchers with disparate backgrounds from around the world to collaborate on innovative open science projects related to brain networks, or connectomes. While the theme sets the type of projects and presentations of the event, it is not meant to be restrictive. Participants are encouraged to post project ideas before via the dedicated form.

An international team of renown experts in the field of network science and neuroscience will coordinate the event in an effort to boost the realization of the projects.

Join the brainhack-networks channel of the Brainhack.org Slack team to stay informed on the upcoming news on the Brainhack Networks content and to contribute with your own ideas and projects.

When?

The 2018 Brainhack Networks will take place just before the Network Neuroscience satellite of the International School and Conference on Network Science (NetSci 2018): June 9-10th, 2018.

Check out the program

Where?

The event will be held in one of the largest hospital in Europe: the Pitié-Salpêtrière hospital, in Paris, FR. And more precisely at the Brain and Spine Institute (ICM).
