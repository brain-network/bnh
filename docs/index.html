NetSci 2018's Brain Networks Hackathon

BNH will be the first of its kind: a hackathon entirely dedicated to brain networks. If you've never heard of it, don't worry. Express tutorials will be given at the very beginning of the event.

More about

The theme of the hackathon is "Network Neuroscience" and will broadly cover a variety of topics related to network science tools for neuroscience/imaging research, including code development, data modeling and analysis, as well as results interpretation.

The goal of Brainhack Networks is to bring together researchers with disparate backgrounds from around the world to collaborate on innovative open science projects related to brain networks. While the theme sets the type of projects and presentations of the event, it is not meant to be restrictive. Participants are encouraged to post project ideas via the registration form. Please join the the SlackBrainhack Networks Slack channel for updated information on the BrainHack Networks content and to contribute with your own ideas.

When?

The Brain Networks Hackathon Networks will take place just before the International School and Conference on Network Science, NetSci18: June 9-10th, 2018.

Check out the program

Where?

The event will be located inside one of the largest hospital in Europe: the Pitié-Salpêtrière hospital, in Paris, FR. And more precisely at the Brain and Spine Institute (ICM).
